Boyle's Law

Description

Boyle's Law states that pressure and volume of gas are inversely proportional so long as the temperature and mass are kept constant.

1.) If the volume of a gas is increased, the pressure of the gas decreases. (T, n Constant)

2.) If the pressure of a gas is decreased, the volume of the gas increases. (T, n Constant)

Applications of the Law

    1.) When you pull the plunger of a syringe, the volume inside increases, causing the pressure to drop. This pressure difference draws the liquid or air into the syringe.

    2.) When you squeeze a balloon, its volume decreases, and the pressure inside increases, illustrating the inverse relationship.

    3.) Pressing the nozzle reduces the volume in the container, increasing pressure and causing the liquid to be expelled as a fine mist.

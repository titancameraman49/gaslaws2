Avogadro's Law

Description

Gay Lussacâ€™s law states that the volume of a gas is directly proportional to the number of moles of gas.

1.) If the volume of a gas is increased, the number of moles of the gas increases. (T, P Constant)

2.) If the number of moles of a gas is decreased, the volume of the gas decreases. (T, P Constant)

Applications of the Law

    1.) When you inhale, your lungs expand to accommodate the increased number of gas molecules (oxygen). Exhaling reduces the number of gas molecules, causing the lung volume to decrease.

    2.) Scuba tanks are filled with compressed air. The number of gas molecules determines the volume of breathable air under water.

    3.) Heating the air inside a balloon causes expansion, increasing volume due to a greater number of active gas molecules (provided external pressure is constant)
